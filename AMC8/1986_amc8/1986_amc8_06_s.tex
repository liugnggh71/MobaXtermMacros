

$ \frac{2}{1-\frac{2}{3}}=$


\[ \textbf{(A)}\ -3 \qquad
\textbf{(B)}\ -\frac{4}{3} \qquad
\textbf{(C)}\ \frac{2}{3} \qquad
\textbf{(D)}\ 2 \qquad
\textbf{(E)}\ 6
\]
\\
Solution
\\
Just simplify the bottom as $\frac{3}{3}-\frac{2}{3}=\frac{1}{3}$, getting us $\frac{2}{\frac{1}{3}}$, with which we multiply top and bottom by 3, we get $\frac{6}{1}$, or $6$
